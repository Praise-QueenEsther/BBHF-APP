Welcome to BBHF

At Blessed to Bless Hands Foundation (BBHF), we exist to make a profound impact on the lives of people, especially in African communities, through unwavering compassion, dedication, and service. We believe in transforming lives, one community at a time, by addressing health, social, economic, educational, communal, and spiritual needs.

History

Our story

Our story is one of inspiration and dedicationâ€”a journey deeply rooted in the belief that every act of kindness has the power to create lasting change, especially in African communities.


Our story began with a profound inspiration drawn from the example set by the Macedonian Church. This ancient church, though not materially rich, was known for its unwavering commitment to giving generously to meet the needs of others. We saw this as a practical and workable approach for addressing the challenges of the 21st century, and we were inspired to act. In response to this inspiration, we founded BBHF with a clear mission: to meet the health, social, economic, educational, communal, and spiritual needs of all people, particularly in African communities. We recognized that these needs were not isolated challenges but interconnected aspects of well-being, and we set out to transform lives, one community at a time.

Our journey has been defined by unwavering dedication to service. We believe in the power of compassion and approach every endeavor with hearts full of empathy and a genuine desire to make a difference. Service is not just a word at BBHF; it's a way of life.


Our belief in the power of collaboration has led us to form partnerships with individuals, corporations, and local communities. Through these alliances, we aim to maximize our impact and reach more people in need. Our story is ongoing, and we invite you to be a part of it. Join us in spreading hope, transforming lives, and making the world a better place.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower individuals and communities to lead self-sustaining and productive lives. We provide enabling opportunities, platforms, and systems for people, particularly youth, to contribute to family, community, and society sustainability and development.

Our Vision

We aim to finance genuine ministries and drive socio-ecomic transformation, especially in Africa. We believe in the power of proactive commitments, partnerships, and the ability of individuals and organizations to create lasting change.

Our objectives

Our guiding principles

At Blessed to Bless Hands Foundation (BBHF), our objectives are the pillars of our mission, encompassing unity, awareness, fundraising, support for missionaries, partnerships, community enlightenment, assistance to needy youths, and the spread of God's goodness. These objectives drive our commitment to creating lasting change, especially in African communities.

Promote Unity

Foster a sense of unity and camaraderie among our members, encouraging a shared commitment to our mission

Raise Awareness

Increase awareness among members about the blessedness of giving into projects that serve the greater good, especially those aligned with our mission.

Fund Kingdom Business

Secure funds to finance and support projects and initiatives dedicated to promoting the well-being of communities, particularly in African regions.

Support Missionaries

Extend assistance to missionaries working in remote and troubled areas through financial aid and material resources.

Forge Partnerships

Collaborate with organizations that share a similar vision and values, seeking opportunities for strategic partnerships that amplify our impact.

Interdenominational Program

Organize an annual interdenominational program to enlighten Christians, individuals, and communities about the importance of supporting the gospel and fulfilling civic responsibilities

Assist Needy Students and Youths

Reach out to extremely needy Christian students and youths in society, providing educational and empowerment opportunities.

Spread God's Goodness

Serve as a conduit for sharing the goodness of God with His Church globally and with people, with a particular focus on Africa.
